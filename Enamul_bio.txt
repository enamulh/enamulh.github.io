Enamul Hoque Prince is a postdoctoral researcher at Stanford University, where he works in the Human Computer Interaction group with Maneesh Agrawala. His research interests are in the broad areas of information visualization and visual analytics, human computer interaction, and natural language processing. He is specifically focusing on devising novel visual analytics techniques to explore large information spaces as well as understanding the utility and potential trade-offs of such techniques from the real user’s perspectives. He received Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of British Columbia, under the supervision of Giuseppe Carenini. He was also advised by Tamara Munzner and Raymond T. Ng. In 2016, he worked as a research intern at Tableau Software and as a reserch associate at Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI). His work has appeared in major journals and conferences such as IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (TVCG), ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems (TIIS), ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI), ACM Intelligent User Interfaces (IUI), EuroVis, and ACL.




His research focuses on combining information visualization and human-computer interaction with natural language processing techniques in a synergistic way to help users explore and analyze large amounts of data.

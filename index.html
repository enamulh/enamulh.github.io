About Me

 

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Stanford University, where I work with Maneesh Agrawala. My research focuses on combining information visualization and human-computer interaction with natural language processing to address the challenges of the information overload problem. In particular, I design and develop both text analysis and interactive visualization techniques to explore large amount of data.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of British Columbia, under the supervision of Dr. Giuseppe Carenini. I was also advised by Profs. Tamara Munzner and Raymond T. Ng. Prior to that, I completed my M.Sc. degree at Memorial University of Newfoundland, Canada under the supervision of Dr. Orland Hoeber and Dr. Minglun Gong.
